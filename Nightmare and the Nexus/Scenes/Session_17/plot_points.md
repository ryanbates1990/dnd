# Nagpa Overview (Urzith, the Remembered)
- meant to be the antithesis of the Havrani. Bent on memories as well, it practices memory necromancy. It extracts all it can from the undead and uses them as fuel. It works to understand the memories of small things so that it can understand the memories of big things.. Like the stolen memories of the ravenqueen it keeps housed in a black ichor well in the center of it's lair.
- he hopes to not only rebuild its own lost past, but it wants to steal divine status. Not to become a god, but to become a permanent echo, a being tied into the fabric of memory across the planes. With the power of the stone, it is weaving memories of itself into the very fabric of the universe.

The undead provide dual purpose, not only to fuel it's memory necromancy, but also simply for the fact that he knows the Ravenqueen hates the undead. They are almost like passive aggressive trophies to him.

# Inside the Blisterwood Tree

## Entrance
- gnarled door made from twisting vines and roots.
- The tree itself is a pale white with no foliage, but the occasional red flower

## Spiral Staircase

- circular chamber with spiral walkways that wind upward along the inner walls
- the inner walls are different shades of a deep blue that shimmer and move, like looking into the ocean. You feel like you can almost make out faces
- PC's must make a DC 16 wisdom check (failures become susceptible to echoes from the well later in the fight)
    - failures feel like someone is tampering with their mind, recalling a terrible memory against their will. Be it losing someone, their own failure, could even be seeing a loved one suffer, etc.

## Memory Lab

- transluscent, deep blue caccoons weaved from the flesh of the tree decorate the perimeter of the tree. A black ichor flows into the pods from what looks like a system of veins. The heart from which this ichor flows is coming from a central point in the ceiling.
    - if anything is damaged in the room the offending party is met a spectre with very little form at first, and as it slowly takes shape it moves like liquid trying to find a shape it likes. You can't tell if it can't hold its shape or can't remember its form. 
        - the spectre is meant to be helpful and answer questions. It doesn't remember it's own life other than the mundane, but there is an awareness that the precious memories it held are gone.
    - after the spectre leaves, any other damage to this room results in the players losing memory of why they are here or a harmful ghost emerges. It seeks to steal memory, Int Save DC 13. On failure take d6 of psychic damage and lose a spell slot/rage/etc.

## Levels 4 and 5

- Level 4 is where the shard is, embedded into the ceiling and the heart of the tree, hanging above a well, A deep basin of black ichor and white mist, is a shard of red stone. It is of incredible beauty, pulsating and breathing, as if it has a life of its own. Tendrils occasionally rise of up out of the well and seep into the shard.
    - The well calls to Castor in a familiar voice. If he interacts with the well he meets Charles. Who tells him he did come across a powerful artifact.. but the amulet of lost dreams was also sought by Urzith.
    - This pool is where Charle's memories are, outside he says he can't imagine what kind of stupefied husk of a man is walking about.
    - He pleads him to pull out a memory that can help him. He shows Castor the way.

    - Castor will interact with a young version of the ravenqueen and must see him as worthy.

    `GM Note` if rex ignores, have a spectral face of charles reach up and show himself before slipping back into the pool

after the characters interact with the pool (some may try to reach but memories allude their hands, like schools of small fish)
