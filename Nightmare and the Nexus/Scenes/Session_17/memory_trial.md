🧠 Phase 1: Prove You're Not Just a Thief
"You reach into a memory not your own. Tell me why you deserve it. Speak carefully—this memory has a will."

Castor must speak truthfully (or at least convincingly) about:

- Why he seeks Charles
- What he hopes to do with the memory (will you protect my memories)
- She can tell Castor has brought death to many. How do you judge who lives and dies?

Mechanics (choose one):

DC 15 Charisma (Persuasion or Deception)

- If Castor speaks sincerely about love, loss, or justice → automatic success
- If Castor is flippant or evasive → Raven Queen grows angry, adds danger to next phase

🗡️ Phase 2: Confront a Fragment of Her Sorrow
Lyessa shows him a memory of losing her friends/family to plague, sought to bring them back with dark magic.. Became skilledm, skilled enough to reach the upper planes. Thinking that paradise is where they would be. Amassing the power to do this made other mage's jealous. Their betrayal disrupted her ascension. You see her lashing out at her peers, placing massive curses on them, transforming them into the Nagpa.

Then she says:

“Would you bear this pain? Carry what I carry?”

- Castor must endure the psychic weight of divine memory.

Mechanics:

`DC 16` Constitution Saving Throw

Success: He takes 2d6 psychic and stands firm.

Fail: He takes 4d6 psychic and must choose:

- Drop the memory and leave
- Accept a Shadow Mark (minor flaw or burden—see below)

🧩 Phase 3: Be Judged by the Raven
Lyessa shifts, feathers turning black and eyes going white. She stands as The Raven Queen, and whispers:

“Then speak. Tell me a moment when you stood between death and life. And if you failed… why should I grant you another chance?”

Castor must narrate (or invent) a moment of mortal consequence—his choice, his flaw, his will to change.

Reward for great roleplay: no roll required.

If needed:

DC 17 Wisdom Save or Charisma Check

Failure: She brands him with a burden (see “Shadow Mark”) but still allows the memory to be taken—barely.

🎁 Reward: The Raven's Memory
A specter of the Raven Queen emerges, screeching in fury and targeting Urzith directly.

“I remember who I was. You would twist that? Then remember this, Urzith.”

😈 Optional Twist: Shadow Mark
If Castor fails a trial but presses on, the Raven Queen leaves a mark—a narrative or mechanical cost.

Examples:

Eyes of Dust: Once per long rest, Castor may reroll a failed death save—but he sees shadows instead of light for 1 minute after.

Burdened Memory: He remembers something horrible that was never his. Permanent -1 to passive Insight, but resistance to being charmed.

Divine Curse: His dreams are haunted by the death of gods. Disadvantage on sleep-based effects, but immune to dream scrying.
